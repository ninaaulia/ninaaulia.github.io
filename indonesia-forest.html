Indonesia Forest Loss and Deforestation

2010–2025 Analysis Using OWID API

This project explores forest loss and deforestation trends in Indonesia from 2010 to 2025 using open datasets from the Our World in Data API.

  • Forest area trends
  • Forest composition (planted vs naturally regenerating)
  • Annual forest area change
  • Drivers of tree cover loss
  • Wildfire-related forest loss
  • Deforestation-related carbon emissions

Indonesia’s Forest Area Since 2010

Indonesia’s forest area declined slightly from 96.31 million hectares in 2010 to 95.97 million hectares in 2025.

Tracking Forest Types

Planted forests increased steadily from 4.5 to 5.5 million hectares, while naturally regenerating forests remained stable around 90 million hectares between 2010 and 2025.

Section 04

Annual Forest Change

Indonesia experienced net forest loss in the early 2010s, followed by a shift toward modest and more stable forest gains since 2016.

Section 05

Drivers of Tree Cover Loss in Indonesia

Permanent agriculture is the largest driver of tree cover loss in Indonesia, consistently accounting for the majority of forest conversion across 2010–2024.

Logging and shifting cultivation are secondary contributors, while wildfires spiked sharply in 2016 before subsiding.

Source: Our World in Data — Drivers of Tree Cover Loss
